## The Misses (And Boy, Were There Misses)

**The Packing Guide That Nobody Asked For:**

"12 Secret Packing Tricks That 90% of Travelers Desperately Want" was... not it. I tried to replicate the ChatGPT tricks article format with packing tips. It bombed.

Lesson learned: **You can't force a format onto a topic that doesn't fit.** Also, nobody desperately wants packing advice from me.

**The AWS Article That Was Too Inside Baseball:**

The AWS outage piece did okay, but not great. I think I went too technical and lost people who weren't already in the cloud computing world.

**Every Time I Tried to Game the Algorithm:**

Any article where I tried to optimize for SEO over honesty? Crickets. Every. Single. Time.

The algorithm doesn't reward clever tricks. It rewards content that people actually want to read and share. Took me way too long to internalize that.

## The Biggest Lesson: Community Over Clicks

Here's the thing I didn't expect: the articles that performed best weren't the ones with the clickiest titles or the most SEO optimization.

They were the ones where I was honest about:
- Not knowing what I'm doing
- Making mistakes publicly
- Sharing real numbers (even embarrassing ones)
- Calling out BS when I see it
- Treating readers like intelligent humans, not engagement metrics

The [Medium claps article](https://wayfinder.page/posts/medium-claps/) where I worried about breaking my expensive mouse? That resonated because it was real.

The [Friends of Medium piece](https://wayfinder.page/posts/medium-friend/) about supporting writers? People connected with the community-first approach.

The [30-second rule article](https://wayfinder.page/posts/medium-30-second-rule/)? It helped people understand how the platform actually works, not how we wish it worked.

And the newest one about [gifting Medium memberships](https://wayfinder.page/posts/gift-medium-membership/)? That's about recognizing the people who show up for you. Community, not commerce.

## What I'm Taking Into 2026

**More experiments, more honesty, less optimization:**

I'm done trying to game the system. The only "hack" that works is writing stuff people actually want to read and being honest about the process.

**The pay-it-forward mentality:**

This showed up in the gift membership article, but it's bigger than that. I want to build a community where we lift each other up, not compete for scraps.

**Shorter cycles between idea and execution:**

The best articles were the ones I wrote quickly, when the idea was fresh. The ones I overthought? Usually worse.

**More newsletters (hopefully on time):**

I said bi-weekly in the first newsletter. I'm zero for one on that promise. But I'm learning. You're learning. We're all just humans trying to make things.
